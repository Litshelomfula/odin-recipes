Lasagne

Description

This is an adaptation of the official lasagne from the Bolognese Chambers of
Commerce. I have made a couple of changes suggested to me by the people
from the area such as, which meat or wine to use or if there should be milk added or
not.One thing never in question,though, is the green lasagne sheets. These are
a must. The ragu is an authentic sauce, simple without garlic or herbs and
spices.Using fresh pasta keeps you from having to pre-boil it.

Ingredients

  • Ragu
  • Pasta
  • Bechamel Sauce

Steps

  1. Saute pancetta into large stockpot until fat has rendered, 1 to 2 minutes.
  2. Stir wine into stockpot. Add tomato sauce and boil.
  3. Pour one more cup of broth into sauce
  4. Start preparing pasta after sauce has been cooking for 1 1/2 hours.
  5. Drain spinach in a mesh strainer over a bowel.
  6. Sift flour into a bowl. Make well in the centre: add eggs, spinach and 1 tablespoon of reserved liquid.
  7. Taste ragu and adjust salt. Remove from the head and add milk.
  8. Divide pasta dough into f4 equal sections
  9. Meanwhile, melt the butter in saucepan over medium heat heat. Add 1/2 cup milk.
  10. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F(175 degrees C)
  11. Spread a little ragu over the base of a 9x13 inch baking dish.Add a layer of pasta. Top with a spoonful of bechamel and sauce.
  12. Bake in oven until cheese has browned and sauce on top is bubbling, for 25 to 30 minutes.
